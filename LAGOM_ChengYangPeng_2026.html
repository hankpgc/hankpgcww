01 — THE PIECE

What is
true happiness?

lagom /ˈlɑːɡɔm/ Swedish — not too much,
not too little, just right

In today's society, we are accustomed to constantly striving for more. But two dancers begin in stillness — settled, present, just right.

Then the morning starts.
They lose it.

They prepare, they perform, they push too hard, they break. LAGOM follows Pipo and Didi through the loss of that stillness — and the long way back to it.

Absurd, tender, and precise. An invitation to return to the present moment.

LAGOM is the first chapter of an ongoing series exploring the question of true happiness.

05 — ARTISTIC VISION

I make work because of what happens in the room when a piece lands.

The moment of connection, of recognition, of someone smiling as they leave. That is why I do this.

I want to bring people together and remind them of something they already know. My work asks one question: What is true happiness? Not the performed kind, but the real kind.

At sixteen I encountered Zen philosophy. It took ten years for that encounter to become clear. Since then my work draws from everything life offers, searching for the concepts that name what we already know but have forgotten.

— Cheng-Yang Peng
